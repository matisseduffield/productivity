# Bento Calendar — Privacy Policy

_Last updated: July 2026_

Bento Calendar is a personal organizer (calendar, notes, tasks). It is built
to keep your data yours.

## What we collect

**Nothing.** Bento Calendar has no accounts, no analytics, no ads, no
tracking, and no servers. The app's developer receives no data from you.

## Where your data lives

Everything you create — events, tasks, notes, settings, and your notes PIN —
is stored **only on your device**, in the app's private storage. Backups you
export are files you choose where to save; they never leave your device
unless you move them yourself.

## Permissions the app uses

- **Notifications** — to show event reminders you set.
- **Alarms** — to deliver those reminders at the exact time.
- **Calendar (read-only, optional)** — only if you enable the device-calendar
  overlay in Settings, the app reads your device's calendars to display them
  alongside your Bento events. It never modifies them, and nothing read is
  stored or transmitted.
- **Internet** — used by the GitHub-distributed build solely to check for app
  updates on GitHub. The Play Store build updates through Google Play. No
  personal data is sent in either case.

## Locked notes

Notes you lock with a PIN are hidden behind that PIN in the app. Exported
backup files contain note content in readable form (your PIN itself is never
exported) — treat backup files like the private documents they are.

## Children

The app is suitable for general audiences and collects no data from anyone.

## Changes

Any change to this policy will appear in this file with an updated date, and
in the app's release notes.
